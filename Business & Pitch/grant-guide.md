# Eloquenta — International Grant & Funding Guide

## By Country

### 🇪🇪 Estonia (Primary HQ)
**Already covered in pitch deck:**
- EdTech Estonia (in-kind, market access)
- EIS Innovation Grant (up to €300K)
- KredEx Start-up Loan (€25K-€50K)
- EU R&D Grants (up to €100K)
- EEA/Norway Grants (€20K-€50K)

**Additional Estonia grants:**
- **EAS (Enterprise Estonia) Export Grant**: Up to €30,000 for market entry costs (travel, trade shows, localization). Requires registered Estonian company.
- **EAS Development Voucher**: €5,000-€15,000 for product development with a research partner.
- **Startup Visa**: Non-EU founders can get a 1-year visa to build in Estonia. No minimum investment.
- **Estonia e-Residency Grant**: €500-€1,000 for first-time e-residents through partner programs.

### 🇩🇪 Germany (Target Market #1)
**Grants for foreign companies establishing in Germany:**
- **EXIST Business Start-up Grant**: Up to €250,000 for innovative startups. Requires a German university partnership. 12-month funding for salary + materials.
- **EXIST Transfer of Research**: Up to €150,000 for commercializing research. Good if partnering with a German university's EdTech lab.
- **KfW Start-up Loan (ERP Startfonds)**: Up to €500,000 at favorable rates. Requires a German business plan and local registration.
- **INVEST Grant for Venture Capital**: 20% tax deduction for angel investors in your German entity. Helps attract German investors.
- **German Federal Ministry of Education (BMBF) EdTech Grants**: €50K-€200K for digital education projects. Requires German-language content.
- **State-level grants (Länder)**: Bavaria, Berlin, Hamburg have their own startup grants (€10K-€50K each).

**Practical note:** Germany requires a GmbH (€25K minimum share capital) or UG (€1, but less credible). You'd need a German director or use a nominee service (~€500/yr).

### 🇯🇵 Japan (Target Market #2)
**Grants for foreign companies:**
- **JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization)**: Free office space in Tokyo/Osaka for 3-6 months, market research support, and introductions to Japanese partners. No equity taken.
- **J-Startup Program**: Selected startups get up to ¥10M (€60K) in funding, mentorship, and access to Japanese corporates. Highly competitive.
- **NEDO (New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization)**: ¥50M-¥200M (€300K-€1.2M) for AI/EdTech projects with Japanese partners.
- **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Startup Fund**: Up to ¥5M (€30K) for companies establishing in Tokyo.
- **Osaka Innovation Hub**: Free co-working + up to ¥3M (€18K) for startups in the Kansai region.

**Practical note:** Japan requires a KK (Kabushiki Kaisha) with ¥1M minimum capital. Foreign directors need a Japanese address or use a virtual office (~¥50K/yr).

### 🇦🇺 Australia (Target Market #3)
**Grants for foreign companies:**
- **Entrepreneurs' Programme**: Up to $1M (€600K) in matched funding for innovative businesses. Requires Australian incorporation.
- **Accelerating Commercialization Grant**: Up to 50% of project costs (max $1M) for bringing new products to market.
- **Export Market Development Grant (EMDG)**: Up to $150,000/year for marketing Australian products/services overseas. Reimburses 50% of eligible export expenses.
- **CSIRO ON Program**: Free access to Australia's national science agency for collaborative R&D. EdTech projects welcome.
- **State grants**: Victoria (up to $50K), NSW (up to $100K), Queensland (up to $75K) all have startup grants.

**Practical note:** Australia requires a Pty Ltd company (no minimum capital). Foreign directors need an Australian director or use a nominee. ASIC registration is ~$500.

### 🇪🇸 Spain (Target Market #4)
**Grants for foreign companies:**
- **ENISA (National Innovation Company)**: €25K-€300K in participative loans for innovative startups. No equity taken. Requires Spanish SL (Sociedad Limitada, €3K minimum capital).
- **CDTI (Centre for Industrial Technological Development)**: Up to €200K for R&D projects. Can be combined with ENISA.
- **ICEX (Spanish Institute of Foreign Trade)**: Up to €50K for market entry and internationalization.
- **Regional grants**: Catalonia (ACCIÓ, up to €100K), Madrid (up to €50K), Basque Country (up to €75K).
- **Startup Law (Ley de Startups)**: €72K tax deduction for investors in Spanish startups. Reduced corporate tax rate of 15% for first 4 years.

**Practical note:** Spain's new Startup Law makes it much easier. You can get a digital nomad visa, and the "Autónomo" regime has reduced social security for first 2 years (€60/mo).

### 🇵🇹 Portugal (Backup HQ option)
**Grants for foreign companies:**
- **Portugal Start-up Visa**: For innovative startups. Requires €5K+ investment and approval from IAPMEI.
- **IAPMEI Grants**: Up to €200K for innovative SMEs. Can be combined with EU funds.
- **Portugal Tech Visa**: Fast-track visa for tech workers. Your teachers could use this.
- **NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) Tax Regime**: 20% flat tax on Portuguese income for 10 years. Great for founders.
- **Startup Portugal +T**: €25K-€100K for early-stage startups.

**Practical note:** Portugal is increasingly popular as an alternative to Estonia. Lower costs, better quality of life, and the NHR tax regime is unbeatable for founders.

### 🇿🇦 South Africa (William's home base)
**Grants for EdTech:**
- **SEDA (Small Enterprise Development Agency)**: Free business support + up to R50K (€2.5K) for startups.
- **SEFA (Small Enterprise Finance Agency)**: Loans up to R5M (€250K) at prime - 3%.
- **TIA (Technology Innovation Agency)**: Up to R5M (€250K) for technology development.
- **IDC (Industrial Development Corporation)**: Up to R10M (€500K) for job-creating businesses.
- **Seda Technology Programme (STP)**: Up to R150K (€7.5K) for tech startups.

**Practical note:** South Africa is the cheapest base for operations. A Pty Ltd costs ~R175 to register. The challenge is banking for international payments — Wise Business works well.

---

### 🇮🇩 Indonesia / Bali (Digital Nomad Hub)
**Grants and incentives:**
- **Bali Digital Nomad Visa (B211A)**: 5-year visa, no tax on foreign income if structured correctly.
- **Indonesia Golden Visa**: 5-10 year visa for investors (minimum $350K investment or $100K for startups).
- **BEKRAF (Creative Economy Agency)**: Up to R500M (€25K) for creative/EdTech startups.
- **No corporate tax for first 2 years** for new companies in special economic zones (Bali Nusa Dua).

**Practical note:** Indonesia requires a PT PMA (foreign-owned company) with minimum $250K investment for most sectors. However, digital services can operate through a local partner structure.

## EU-Wide Grants (Available from any EU entity)

### Horizon Europe
- **EIC Accelerator**: €2.5M grant + €15M equity for breakthrough innovations. Highly competitive but perfect for AI-powered EdTech.
- **EIC Pathfinder**: Up to €3M for early-stage research. Good for the AI lesson generation technology.
- **Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions**: Up to €200K for hiring researchers. Could fund AI/EdTech research staff.

### Erasmus+
- **Erasmus+ Strategic Partnerships**: Up to €450K for cross-border education projects. Perfect for partnering with European universities.
- **Erasmus+ KA2 (Cooperation Partnerships)**: Up to €400K for developing innovative educational content.

### Digital Europe Programme
- **Digital Europe (DIGITAL)**: Up to €2M for AI and digital skills projects. Directly relevant to Eloquenta's AI lesson platform.
- **European Digital Innovation Hubs (EDIHs)**: Free access to testing facilities, expertise, and funding advice across Europe.

### European Social Fund Plus (ESF+)
- **ESF+ Innovation Grants**: Up to €500K for projects that improve digital skills and education access. Available through national managing authorities.

## Key Recommendations

1. **Start with Estonia** — lowest cost, fastest setup, EU credibility. Budget €5,400/yr for compliance.
2. **Apply to EdTech Estonia immediately** — free, in-kind, opens doors.
3. **Use JETRO for Japan entry** — free office space, no equity.
4. **Apply for Erasmus+ in Year 2** — perfect for cross-border education partnerships.
5. **Keep Portugal as backup** — NHR tax regime is unbeatable if Estonia becomes too costly.
6. **Don't ignore South Africa** — SEFA and TIA grants are accessible and William is already based there.
7. **Budget for banking friction** — Wise Business as primary, Estonian bank account as secondary (2-6 week delay expected).
