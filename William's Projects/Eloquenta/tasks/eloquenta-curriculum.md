# Eloquenta Eloquence Levels — Full Curriculum

## LEVEL STRUCTURE (16 micro-levels across 5 bands)

BAND A: FOUNDATION (A1- → A1 → A1+ → A2- → A2 → A2+)
BAND B: INTERMEDIATE (B1- → B1 → B1+ → B2- → B2 → B2+)
BAND C: ADVANCED (C1- → C1 → C1+)

---

## BAND A: FOUNDATION

### A1- — "Sound Starter"
CEFR: Pre-A1 | Focus: Survival English, first sounds, listening

**Language Points:**
- It is / it isn't / is it?
- I am / you are / he is / she is
- Basic nouns: colours, sizes, everyday objects
- How much + numbers up to 20
- There is / there are (basic)

**Vocabulary:** Colours, sizes, numbers 1-20, basic professions, family, prices, office items, food & drink

**Pronunciation Focus:** Vowel sounds, word stress in basic nouns, question intonation (rising)

**At Work:**
- 'Is it a blue cup?' 'No, it's a red cup.'
- 'How much is it?' 'It's 7 euros.'
- 'There is a computer on my desk.'

**Outcomes:** Give basic descriptions. Ask about prices. Identify objects and proximity.

**Placement Test:** Point-and-identify, yes/no questions, name 5+ objects

**Cultural Note:** South African English sounds — "Robot" (traffic light), "Lekker" (nice), "Braai" (barbecue), "Eish" (expression of surprise)

### A1 — "First Contact"
CEFR: A1 | Focus: Introductions, identity, daily routine

**Language Points:**
- To be (affirmative, negative, interrogative) + a/an
- Have/has (positive, negative, interrogative)
- Present simple (I, you) — daily routines
- Possessive adjectives: my, your, his, her
- Interrogative: what, where, when with do/does
- There is/are + some/any
- This, that, these, those
- At + telling the time

**Vocabulary:** Countries, nationalities, professions, family members, rooms, furniture, months, days of the week, telling the time, clothes, hotel hospitality

**Pronunciation Focus:** The English alphabet (spelling out loud), -s/-es plurals, silent letters (knife, Wednesday)

**At Work:**
- 'My name is Francesca. And you?'
- 'I am Italian. She isn't Italian, she is French.'
- 'I get up and then I have a shower.'
- 'Good morning, I would like to check into my hotel room please.'

**Outcomes:** Introduce yourself & others. Explain nationality and profession. Describe daily routine. Check into a hotel. Tell the time.

**Key Test:** Make a full introduction (name, country, profession, family, daily routine)

---

### A1+ — "Social Echo"
CEFR: A1+ | Focus: Conversations, likes/dislikes, making arrangements

**Language Points:**
- To like, love, hate, prefer + gerund
- Can/can't (ability)
- Present continuous (positive, negative, interrogative)
- How often + adverbs of frequency
- On + days of the week / Before/after
- There is/are + prepositions of place (next to, between, under, behind)
- Would like vs. like

**Vocabulary:** Sports & hobbies, food & drink (quantities), entertainment, days of the week, common adjectives

**Pronunciation Focus:** -ing ending, "th" sounds (think, this), contrastive stress ("I LIKE coffee, not tea")

**At Work:**
- 'I like playing football.' 'My sister loves skiing.'
- 'Does your sister like playing football?' 'No, she doesn't.'
- 'I play basketball once a week.'
- 'My wife can speak French.' 'I can't ride a motorbike.'

**Outcomes:** Express likes/dislikes. Ask about abilities. Make social arrangements. Describe what's happening now. Correct someone politely.

**Key Test:** Arrange a social event with a friend (agree day, time, activity)

---

### A2- — "Short Hops"
CEFR: A2- | Focus: Past experiences, counting, ordering

**Language Points:**
- Past simple: regular verbs (introduction)
- Past simple: 10 key irregular verbs (be, go, have, eat, drink, see, buy, take, come, make)
- How much/many + countable/uncountable
- Numbers 101-1000, dates, ordinal numbers
- Imperative + object pronouns
- Possessive adjectives: our, their

**Vocabulary:** Food & drink (restaurant), travel, shopping, dates & months, ordinals, restaurant ordering, basic maths

**Pronunciation Focus:** -ed endings (walked /t/, wanted /ɪd/, played /d/), "th" vs /t/ vs /d/

**At Work:**
- 'Last week I started on a new project.'
- 'Last summer I went to Sardinia and I ate fish and salad.'
- 'Good afternoon, I would like to order...'
- 'How much fruit do you eat a week?' 'I eat quite a lot.'

**Outcomes:** Talk about past events. Order in a restaurant. Handle bigger numbers and dates. Make basic work requests.

**Key Test:** Tell a story about a past trip or meal in 5+ sentences

---

### A2 — "Bridge Builder"
CEFR: A2 | Focus: Plans, comparisons, obligation, future

**Language Points:**
- Future with "going to" and "will"
- Comparative adjectives (bigger, more expensive)
- Must, should, mustn't, shouldn't (obligation)
- Superlative adjectives (the biggest, the best)
- Have to / don't have to
- The Saxon Genitive ('s)
- Past continuous introduction

**Vocabulary:** Countries (comparison), weather, shopping, travel, social activities

**Pronunciation Focus:** "th" in "the/this/that/there", stress in comparative forms (exPENsive vs CHEAPer)

**At Work:**
- 'Next weekend I am going to play basketball.'
- 'I'll go to the cinema if something good is on.'
- 'Portugal is smaller than Germany.'
- 'If you visit my city, you must go to the stadium.'
- 'The best place to eat is near the main square.'

**Outcomes:** Talk about future plans. Compare places and things. Give advice. Express obligation. Describe weather and shopping.

**Key Test:** Plan and describe a future trip itinerary

---

### A2+ — "Connected Flow"
CEFR: A2+ | Focus: Experiences, direction, travel narratives

**Language Points:**
- Present perfect introduction: Have you ever...? / Never / Already / Yet / Just
- Past simple vs. Present perfect (introduction)
- Directions: turn left/right, go straight, next to, opposite
- Something/someone vs. anything/anyone
- To be born (stative)
- Phrasal verbs basics: take off, put on, pick up, put down

**Vocabulary:** Travel, directions, life events, everyday objects

**Pronunciation Focus:** Present perfect contractions (I've, you've, he's), linking in connected speech

**At Work:**
- 'Have you ever been to Japan?' 'No, I have never been.'
- 'To get to the stadium, turn left here and continue to the roundabout.'
- 'My grandfather was born in 1925.'
- 'Pick up the pen — pick it up.' 'Take off your glasses — take them off.'

**Outcomes:** Talk about life experiences. Give and follow directions. Use basic phrasal verbs. Distinguish past experiences from specific past events.

**Key Test:** Describe your life experiences and give directions to a visitor

## BAND B: INTERMEDIATE

### B1- — "Story Stringer"
CEFR: B1- | Focus: Narratives, media, reporting

**Language Points:**
- Past simple irregular verbs (30 verbs) — review and expand
- Past continuous + "when" and "while"
- Reported speech introduction (present → past)
- Passive voice: present, past, future (basic)
- Whose + possessive review

**Vocabulary:** Travel, media, crime, news, daily events

**Pronunciation Focus:** Weak forms in connected speech (was /wəz/, were /wə/), "ed" review

**At Work:**
- 'She was jogging in the park when she saw the robbery.'
- 'When we went to Greece, we flew by Ryanair and drove around the islands.'
- 'My mother's brother is a doctor.'
- 'They said they were going to do some research.'

**Outcomes:** Tell detailed stories using past tenses. Report what someone said. Use passive voice in basic forms. Describe ongoing past events.

**Key Test:** Retell a news story or personal event using all past tenses

---

### B1 — "Full Circle"
CEFR: B1 | Focus: Opinions, arguments, work communication

**Language Points:**
- Present perfect vs. past simple (contrast with time markers)
- Present perfect continuous + how long / for / since
- Used to (past habits)
- First conditional + when/until
- Comparative with "as...as"
- Would like + infinitive
- Verb + infinitive vs. verb + gerund (contrast)

**Vocabulary:** Work, education, life changes, deadlines, scheduling, opinions

**Pronunciation Focus:** Intonation for expressing opinions (falling for certainty, rising for doubt), "used to" /juːst tə/

**At Work:**
- 'Yesterday I ran 5kms. Today I have run 2kms.'
- 'I have been living here since 2012.'
- 'I used to revise for two hours before an exam.'
- 'If it's sunny this weekend, I'll go to the beach.'

**Outcomes:** Contrast completed vs. ongoing time frames. Describe past habits vs. present. Express opinions and conditions. Talk about duration. Make plans and promises.

**Key Test:** Ask for support for a project or idea — explain timeline, needed resources, and expected outcome

---

### B1+ — "Content Weaver"
CEFR: B1+ | Focus: Persuasion, work idioms, professional communication

**Language Points:**
- Second conditional (hypothetical)
- Superlatives review (the most... / the -est)
- Too / too much / too many / enough
- Linking words: however, although, in addition, unless
- Questions with prepositions at the end
- Indirect questions

**Vocabulary:** Business negotiation, work phrasal verbs, social issues, complaints

**Pronunciation Focus:** Stress-timed speech, connected speech in conditionals

**At Work:**
- 'If I went to work in another country, I would choose Australia.'
- 'The hotel room is too noisy. There is too much traffic outside.'
- 'What is your boss in charge of?'
- 'Can you tell me what your name is?' vs. 'What is your name?'

**Outcomes:** Discuss hypothetical situations. Make complaints and negotiate. Use indirect questions politely. Link ideas with connectors. End questions naturally with prepositions.

**Key Test:** Deliver a short persuasive pitch (product, idea, or proposal)

---

### B2- — "Deeper Roots"
CEFR: B2- | Focus: Complex past, modals of deduction, relative clauses

**Language Points:**
- Past perfect (action before another past action)
- Modal verbs for deduction: must be / can't be / might be
- Modal verbs for past: should have / could have / would have
- Relative clauses: defining and non-defining (who, which, whose, that, when, where)
- Passive voice review with modals

**Vocabulary:** Money (mortgage, transaction, debt), investigation, research, crime, education

**Pronunciation Focus:** Reduced forms (must've, would've, hadn't), emphasis with "do/does" in affirmative sentences

**At Work:**
- 'I had researched mortgage deals before I made the offer on the house.'
- 'The man must be a policeman — he is wearing the uniform.'
- 'They can't be at home — the lights are off.'
- 'Name me a business whose logo is yellow.'

**Outcomes:** Talk about events before other past events. Express deductions about a situation. Link sentences with relative modals. Give indirect commands with modals.

**Key Test:** Argue a point using modals of deduction and past counterfactuals ("If the company had invested earlier...")

---

### B2 — "Nuanced Navigator"
CEFR: B2+ | Focus: Precision, register, formal/informal, debate

**Language Points:**
- Third conditional (impossible past)
- Mixed conditionals (past condition → present result)
- Wish/If only (present and future)
- Quantifiers: so much/many, so, too, enough
- Verbs + gerund (advanced list: avoid, risk, regret, resist, tolerate, consider, miss, involve)
- Verbs + infinitive (advanced list: agree, appear, promise, afford, manage, refuse, hope, expect, deserve, fail)
- Verbs that change meaning: try + gerund/infinitive, stop, remember, forget
- As if / as though

**Vocabulary:** Personality appearance, senses, complex emotions, debate topics

**Pronunciation Focus:** Stress and intonation for emphasis, rhetorical pauses, formal vs. informal register in delivery

**At Work:**
- 'If I had caught a taxi this morning instead of a bus, I would have arrived on time.'
- 'If I had studied economics instead of chemistry, I would work in the city now.'
- 'I wish you wouldn't leave the window open at night.'
- 'He failed to arrange the meeting at the bank.'

**Outcomes:** Discuss impossible alternative pasts. Express regrets about past and present. Debate complex topics with nuanced language. Use a wider range of verb patterns. Distinguish formal from informal register.

**Key Test:** Influence a team toward a specific action — justify, persuade, and respond to objections

---

### B2+ — "Influence Artist"
CECEFR: B2+ | Focus: Leadership, influence, negotiation

**Language Points:**
- All conditionals review and mixing
- Unless / as long as / even if + conditionals
- Reported speech (all tenses)
- Wish/If only review (all tenses)
- Neither/nor, either/or
- Future perfect + future tenses review

**Vocabulary:** Business negotiation phrases, societal issues, economics, professional development

**Pronunciation Focus:** Advanced intonation for influence — tag questions, rhetorical questions, persuasive emphasis

**At Work:**
- 'Even if there were a meeting this afternoon, I wouldn't go.'
- 'She asked me if I had completed the report.'
- 'If only it would stop raining.'
- 'I hope to make head of department in the next three years.'
- 'By the time I am 40, I will have saved enough to start my own business.'

**Outcomes:** Hypothetical outcomes in negotiations. Use all conditionals fluidly. Relay others' words accurately. Express desires about past, present, and future. Talk about future events before other events.

**Key Test:** Lead a negotiation scenario — make proposals, handle objections, reach agreement

## BAND C: ADVANCED

### C1- — "Expert Explorer"
CEFR: C1- | Focus: Sophisticated expression, idioms, collocations, complex topics

**Language Points:**
- All passive voice tenses with "to be" review
- Modal verbs: obligation/deduction in the past (should have, must have, may have)
- All forms of "there" (there must be, there used to be, there has been)
- Anglo-Saxon suffixes: -less, -ship, -ness (word building)
- Advanced collocations: take (risk, place, hold, advantage), make (one's way, a point, ends meet)

**Vocabulary:** Economics, politics, media & marketing, environment, science

**Pronunciation Focus:** Near-native intonation patterns, connected speech mastery, thought groups and pausing

**At Work:**
- 'The package must be sent tonight.'
- 'He must have been tired — he had just run a marathon.'
- 'By the time I retire, I will have been working for 40 years.'
- 'What does it take to run a company, do you think?'
- 'A parade takes place in my home town every year.'

**Outcomes:** Debate economics, politics, environment, media. Use idioms naturally. Build words from roots and suffixes. Understand and discuss complex written and spoken texts. Write structured business communications.

**Key Test:** Debate a complex societal topic (income inequality, AI impact, environmental policy) for 5+ minutes with nuanced arguments

---

### C1 — "Command Crafter"
CEFR: C1 | Focus: Complex grammar as natural speech, extended discourse

**Language Points:**
- Passive with modal verbs (must be done, could have been done, would be done)
- Would rather / Had better
- Likely/unlikely + likelihood
- Be supposed to
- Get + participle (passive alternative: get paid, get promoted, get stolen)
- All conditionals mixed and natural use

**Vocabulary:** Home improvement/DIY, film/restaurant reviews, charts & graphs, body gestures, societal issues, renting, car repair

**Pronunciation Focus:** Natural speech rhythm, schwa usage, linking, elision, assimilation

**At Work:**
- 'The project must be done by Friday. It should have been done yesterday.'
- 'I would rather fit a new front door than install new windows.'
- 'I get paid at the end of the month.' 'His car got stolen.'
- 'The movie was supposed to be good — it had great reviews.'
- 'The company experienced steady growth in the early part of the year, but then it started to fluctuate.'

**Outlays:** Maintain a single train of thought for 5+ minutes. Use complex grammar naturally. Describe charts and data. Discuss societal issues in depth. Handle everyday problems (renting, car repair, medical) in English. Spell non-phonetic words accurately.

**Key Test:** Deliver a 5-minute presentation on a business topic with data, proposals, and Q&A handling

---

### C1+ — "Distinguished Speaker"
CEFR: C1+ | Focus: Mastery, near-native fluency, leadership communication

**Language Points:**
- All grammar reviewed at mastery level
- Less common connectors: nevertheless, therefore, in spite of, besides, in fact
- Concessive clauses: whatever, wherever, whoever, whenever, whichever, however
- Distinctions: come back/go back, rise/raise, take/bring, none/no/any
- Less common phrasal verbs (pop up, set out, go over, buy out, sell out, drop off, head off)
- Idioms collection: cost an arm and a leg, better late than never, that ship has sailed, make ends meet, cross that bridge, cut corners, kill two birds with one stone

**Vocabulary:** Professional idioms, compound adjectives (mind-blowing, heart-breaking, game-changing, mind-numbing, freaked-out), media, marketing

**Pronunciation Focus:** Delivery mastery — pacing, pausing for effect, humor, storytelling cadence, audience engagement

**At Work:**
- 'Whatever challenges we face, I will get to that meeting on time.'
- 'Even now, I am feeling better than ever.'
- 'This technology is game-changing in my industry.'
- 'I'm not much of a practical joker, although there was this one time...'
- 'Words that have entered the dictionary in the last 10 years include "to google," "to whatsapp," and even "YOLO."'

**Outcomes:** Speak at near-native fluency. Tell jokes and funny stories. Deliver compelling presentations. Debate any topic with precision. Write professional emails and reports. Understand cultural references and evolving language (slang, new words, social media English). Mentor other learners.

**Key Test:** Deliver a 10-minute TED-talk-style presentation followed by handling audience questions with fluency and poise
