🏛️ Politics & Policy

8 questions
Tariffs are an effective policy for nations to protect local industries. To what extent do you agree or disagree? B2
▼
Key Vocabulary
tariffprotectionismfree tradedomestic industryimport dutytrade warsubsidyWTO
Discussion Angles
Agree: Tariffs protect infant industries, preserve jobs, and give domestic companies time to compete. Developing nations especially need this protection.
Disagree: Tariffs raise consumer prices, invite retaliation, and create inefficiency. Free trade ultimately benefits everyone through specialization.
Both: Short-term tariffs can be useful for strategic industries (defense, food security) but long-term protectionism breeds complacency.
Follow-up Questions
  • Should developing countries have different tariff rules than developed ones?
  • Can you think of a tariff that backfired?
  • How do tariffs affect the average consumer?
Social media algorithms pose a greater threat to democracy than state censorship. To what extent do you agree or disagree? C1
▼
Key Vocabulary
algorithmecho chamberfilter bubblecensorshipdisinformationpolarizationengagement metric
Discussion Angles
Agree: Algorithms are invisible — people don't know they're being manipulated. State censorship is at least visible and can be protested.
Disagree: State censorship is enforced by law and violence. You can install an ad blocker; you can't install a "freedom blocker."
Both: They work together — authoritarian governments use social media algorithms as their primary censorship tool.
Follow-up Questions
  • Should governments regulate social media algorithms?
  • Is it possible to have a truly "neutral" algorithm?
  • How has social media changed elections in your country?

Universal Basic Income (UBI) should be implemented in all developed countries. To what extent do you agree or disagree? B2
▼
Key Vocabulary
universal basic incomewelfare stateautomationinflationmeans testingfiscal policy
Discussion Angles
Agree: Automation will eliminate millions of jobs. UBI provides a safety net and frees people to pursue education, entrepreneurship, or caregiving.
Disagree: UBI is unaffordable at scale, reduces work incentives, and is a blunt instrument. Targeted welfare is more efficient.
Follow-up Questions
  • What would happen to inflation if everyone received free money?
  • Has any UBI pilot program shown positive results?
  • Should UBI replace existing welfare programs or supplement them?

Discussion Angles

Disagree: The gig economy offers flexibility that many workers value. Over-regulation could destroy the model and reduce opportunities.
Follow-up Questions
  • Should gig workers be classified as employees?
  • How does the gig economy affect your country's labor market?
  • What protections should gig workers have without killing the model?

▼
Key Vocabulary
sustainabilitygreenwashingbuzzwordcorporate social responsibilityfatigue
Discussion Angles
Agree: The word has been overused and diluted. Companies use it for marketing without real action. People have become desensitized to it.
Disagree: The concept is more important than ever. Just because a word is overused doesn't mean the underlying issue has been solved.
Follow-up Questions
  • What word should replace "sustainability"?
  • Can you think of a company that practices real sustainability vs. greenwashing?
  • How do you feel when a company says they're "committed to sustainability"?

▼
Key Vocabulary
immigrationintegrationlabor shortagebrain drainxenophobiaremittances
Discussion Angles
Agree: Immigrants fill labor gaps, start businesses, pay taxes, and enrich culture. Aging populations need young workers.
Disagree: Rapid immigration strains public services, depresses wages for low-skilled workers, and can create social tension if integration fails.
Follow-up Questions
  • Should immigration be based on skills, family reunification, or humanitarian need?
  • How does immigration affect your country?
  • What's the difference between a refugee and an economic migrant?

gender pay gapdiscriminationunconscious biasmotherhood penaltynegotiationtransparency
Discussion Angles
Discrimination: Women are paid less for the same work. They're steered away from high-paying fields. The "motherhood penalty" is real.

Discussion Angles
Agree: Climate change causes migration, conflict over resources, and infrastructure collapse. Military planners already treat it as a threat multiplier.

Discussion Angles
Agree: Nuclear provides reliable, low-carbon baseline power. Renewables alone can't meet demand. New reactor designs are safer.

  • Would you live near a nuclear power plant?
