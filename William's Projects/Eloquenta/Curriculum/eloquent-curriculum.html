Band A — Foundation (6 Levels)
A1-✦
Pre-A1 · Sound Starter

First SoundsSurvival English & Listening

Vowel sounds · Word stress · Question intonation
▾
~40 words
5 min speaking
Goal: Survive basic exchanges

You Can

  • Point to objects and name them
  • Ask and answer "What is this?"
  • Say prices and count to 20
  • Use "this/that/these/those"
  • Identify singular vs. plural

Phonetics Focus

Vowel sounds (ship vs sheep, cat vs cut)

Word stress in basic nouns (WA-ter, com-PU-ter)

Rising intonation for questions

Key Phrases

"What is this?"
"How much is it?"
"There is / there are"
"It's 7 euros."

A1✦
A1 · First Contact

IntroductionsIdentity & Daily Life

To be · Have/has · Present simple · Telling time
▾
~120 words
2 min speaking
Goal: Introduce yourself fully

You Can

  • Introduce yourself and others
  • Say where you're from and your job
  • Describe your daily routine
  • Tell the time and make arrangements
  • Ask about someone's day

Phonetics Focus

The alphabet — spelling names clearly

-s/-es endings (/s/ vs /z/ vs /ɪz/)

Silent letters (knife, Wednesday)

Key Phrases

"My name is... I'm from... I work as..."
"I usually get up at..."
"Let's meet on Tuesday at 3."

A1+✦
A1+ · Social Echo

ConversationsOpinions, Likes & Arrangements

Like/love/hate + gerund · Can/can't · Present continuous
▾
~200 words total
3 min speaking
Goal: Have a basic social conversation

You Can

  • Express likes, dislikes, and preferences
  • Talk about abilities (I can swim / I can't drive)
  • Describe what's happening now
  • Make social plans and agree on details
  • Respond naturally in conversation

Phonetics Focus

-ing ending (/ɪŋ/ not /ɪng/)

th sounds: think /θ/ vs this /ð/

Contrastive stress: "I LIKE coffee, not tea"

Key Phrases

"I like playing..." / "I can't swim but..."
"How about Wednesday at 7?"
"Sounds good! Where should we meet?"

A2-✦
A2- · Short Hops

Stories & NumbersPast Events, Dates & Quantities

Past simple (regular & irregular) · How much/many · Numbers to 1000
▾
~350 words total
5 min speaking
Goal: Tell a story about the past

You Can

  • Talk about past events using correct verbs
  • Use numbers 101-1000, dates, and ordinals
  • Ask about quantities (how much/many)
  • Give instructions and make requests
  • Order food in a restaurant

Phonetics Focus

-ed endings: walked /t/, wanted /ɪd/, played /d/

Connected speech: "did you" → /dɪdʒu/

Key Phrases

"Last weekend I went to..."
"How much fruit do you eat a week?"
"I'd like to order the steak, please."

A2✦
A2 · Bridge Builder

Plans & ComparisonsFuture, Obligation & Advice

Going to / Will · Comparatives · Must/should · Superlatives
▾
~500 words total
7 min speaking
Goal: Discuss plans and compare options

You Can

  • Talk about future plans and intentions
  • Compare places, things, and experiences
  • Give advice and express obligation
  • Use superlatives (the best, the worst)
  • Describe weather and shopping

Key Phrases

"I'm going to visit..." / "I'll probably..."
"Portugal is smaller than Germany."
"You should try the local food."

A2+✦
A2+ · Connected Flow

Experiences & DirectionsPresent Perfect, Travel & Phrasal Verbs

Have you ever? · Already/yet/just · Directions · Phrasal verbs
▾
~700 words total
10 min speaking
Goal: Share life experiences fluently

You Can

  • Talk about life experiences (Have you ever?)
  • Use already, yet, and just correctly
  • Give and follow directions
  • Use basic phrasal verbs (take off, put on)
  • Describe where you were born

Key Phrases

"Have you ever been to Japan?"
"Turn left at the roundabout."
"I've already finished the report."

▾
~1400 words total
~30 min speaking
Goal: Express opinions with nuance

You Can

  • Compare with "as...as"
  • Express opinions with reasons

Phonetics Focus

Intonation: falling (certainty) vs rising (doubt)

"used to" /juːst tə/
